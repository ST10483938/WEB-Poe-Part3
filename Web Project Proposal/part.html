Our Programs & Initiatives

Explore our diverse programs designed to educate, engage, and empower our community.

Community Garden Plots
Rent your own garden plot and grow fresh vegetables, herbs, and flowers. Our 4x8 ft plots come with water access, organic soil, and expert guidance. Perfect for families, individuals, or groups wanting to experience the joy of growing their own food. Monthly plot rental: $25/month (discounts available for seniors and students).
Gardening Workshops
Join our monthly workshops covering topics like organic pest control, composting, seed saving, seasonal planting, and permaculture design. Led by experienced gardeners and agricultural experts. All skill levels welcome! Workshops are held every second Saturday at 10 AM. Free for members, $10 for non-members.
Youth Education Program
Our youth program introduces children ages 6-16 to gardening, nutrition, and environmental stewardship. Through hands-on activities, kids learn where food comes from, the importance of biodiversity, and gain valuable life skills. After-school programs run Tuesday and Thursday, 3-5 PM. Summer camp available in July and August.
Food Security Initiative
We donate 30% of our harvest to local food banks and families in need. Our community meals program serves fresh, garden-grown food every Friday evening. We believe everyone deserves access to healthy, nutritious produce regardless of their financial situation. Volunteers always welcome!
Sustainability & Composting
Our composting program diverts over 2 tons of organic waste from landfills annually. Drop off your kitchen scraps at our compost station (open daily 7 AM - 7 PM) and receive finished compost for your garden. We also offer rainwater harvesting workshops and maintain a seed library with heirloom varieties.
